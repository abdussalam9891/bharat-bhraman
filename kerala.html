Overview

Kerala, India’s tropical paradise, offers a rare blend of nature, comfort, and calm. This 7-day curated journey covers tea estates in Munnar, spice plantations in Thekkady, and a private houseboat stay in Alleppey’s backwaters. Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1 Arrival in Kochi — Colonial Charm ▼
Arrive in Kochi and transfer to your hotel. Explore Fort Kochi — Chinese fishing nets, colonial streets, and seaside cafés. Evening Kathakali performance (optional). Overnight in Kochi.
Day 2 Kochi → Munnar — Into the Hills ▼
Scenic drive to Munnar (approx. 4–5 hrs) through waterfalls and tea estates. Stop at viewpoints along the way. Check in and relax in the cool mountain air. Overnight in Munnar.
Day 3 Munnar — Tea Gardens & National Park ▼
Visit Eravikulam National Park (home of Nilgiri Tahr), tea plantations, and Mattupetty Dam. Enjoy panoramic views of rolling green hills. Overnight in Munnar.
Day 4 Munnar → Thekkady — Spice & Wildlife ▼
Drive to Thekkady (approx. 3–4 hrs). Visit spice plantations and learn about Kerala’s famous spices. Optional evening cultural show or martial arts performance. Overnight in Thekkady.
Day 5 Thekkady → Alleppey — Backwater Houseboat ▼
Drive to Alleppey and board a private houseboat. Cruise through serene backwaters, passing villages, coconut groves, and paddy fields. Enjoy meals onboard. Overnight on houseboat.
Day 6 Alleppey → Kochi — Leisure Day ▼
Disembark after breakfast and drive back to Kochi. Day at leisure for shopping or relaxing by the coast. Overnight in Kochi.
Day 7 Departure ▼
Transfer to Kochi airport or railway station for your onward journey. Tour ends with calm memories of Kerala.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about visiting Kerala

What is the best time to visit Kerala? ▼
October to March is the best time for pleasant weather and sightseeing. Monsoon (June to September) is ideal for Ayurvedic treatments and lush greenery, while summer months are warmer but still suitable for hill stations like Munnar.
Are houseboat stays included in the package? ▼
Yes, most Kerala tour packages include a private houseboat stay in Alleppey. It includes meals onboard and a relaxing cruise through the backwaters.
Is Kerala suitable for family trips and couples? ▼
Absolutely. Kerala is perfect for families, couples, and honeymooners. It offers a mix of nature, relaxation, and cultural experiences suitable for all age groups.
Can I customize my Kerala itinerary? ▼
Yes, all our Kerala tours are fully customizable. You can adjust destinations, hotel categories, and activities based on your preferences and budget.
What kind of clothes should I pack for Kerala? ▼
Light cotton clothes are recommended due to the tropical climate. Carry comfortable footwear, sunscreen, and a light jacket if visiting hill stations like Munnar.

A small advance is required to confirm your booking, with the remaining amount payable before or during the trip. Full payment details are shared transparently at booking.
